    NMT Case 8

    USA vs. Ulrich Greifelt, et al.

    Summary

    Trial VIII, The United States of America vs. Ulrich Greifelt, et al, also known as the RuSHA trial, took place between July 1947 and March 1948; appeal of sentences continued through 1949. Fourteen defendants were charged: Ulrich Greifelt, Rudolf Creutz, Konrad Meyer-Hetling, Otto Schwarzenberger, Herbert Huebner, Werner Lorenz, Heinz Brueckner, Otto Hofmann, Richard Hildebrandt, Fritz Schwalm, Max Sollmann, Gregor Ebner, Guenther Tesch, and Inge Viermetz. During the trial, the prosecution introduced 904 written exhibits and called 27 witnesses; the defense introduced 1148 written exhibits and called 70 witnesses. The tribunal convened 121 times.

    The defendants were leading officials of "RuSHA", the Race and Settlement Main Office of the SS (SS-Rasse- und Siedlungshauptamt), or of three other agencies: the Staff Main Office of the Reich Commissioner for the Strengthening of Germandom (Stabshauptamt des Reichskommissars fuer die Festigung des deutschen Volkstums, or RKFDV), the Office for Repatriation of Ethnic Germans (Volksdeutsche Mittelstelle, or VoMi), and the "Lebensborn" ("Well of Life"), which was both a private association and part of the personal staff of Heinrich Himmler. The crimes charged to the defendants were connected with a systematic program of genocide. In its judgment, the Tribunal declared that these organizations existed for the primary purpose of "weakening and eventually destroying other nations while at the same time strengthening Germany, territorially and biologically, at the expense of conquered nations."

    Indictment

    The indictment had three counts. Defendants were accused of the following as a direct consequence of their connection to the organizations named above:

    Count One: crimes against humanity, including

    • kidnapping children of foreign nationals
    • compelling abortions on Eastern workers
    • taking away infants born to Eastern workers
    • punishment for sexual intercourse with Germans
    • hampering reproduction of enemy nationals
    • forced evacuation and resettlement of populations
    • forced Germanization of enemy nationals
    • slave labor
    • conscription of non-Germans
    • plunder of public and private property
    • persecution and extermination of Jews

    Count Two: war crimes

    • Plunder
    • murder
    • extermination
    • enslavement
    • deportation
    • imprisonment, torture, ill-treatment

    Count Three: membership in criminal organizations (SS): all defendants except Viermetz

    Abbreviations/Terms/Persons/Organizations Mentioned in Trial

    RuSHA: Rasse- und Siedlungshauptamt, Race and Settlement Main Office of the SS

    RKFDV: Reichskommisar fuer die Festigung des deutschen Volkstums, Reich Commissioner for the Strengthening of Germandom

    VoMi: Volksdeutsche Mittelstelle, Repatriation Office for Ethnic Germans

    EWZ: Einwandererzentralstelle, an agency that regulated the naturalization and settlement of ethnic Germans

    UWZ: Umwandererzentralstelle, an agency that coordinated expulsion of Poles, Ukrainians and Jews from parts of Poland annexed by the Reich, and that resettled ethnic Germans into these annexed territories

    WED: Wiedereindeutschung, re-Germanization

    Wiedergutmachung: compensation

    DVL: Deutsche Volksliste (German Peoples List)

    Polen-Vermoegensordnung: Polish property decree

    VDA: Verein Deutschtum in Ausland

    DAG: Deutsche Ansiedlungsgesellschaft: German Settlement Company

    HTO: Haupttreuhandstelle OST, Main Trusteeship Office in East (agency involved in seizing goods of Jews and Poles)

    DUT: Deutsche Umsiedlungs-Treuhand-Gesellschaft, German Resettlement Trust Society

    CdZ: Chef der Zivilverwaltung, Chief of the Civil Administration

    Reichsland G.m.b.H aka Ostland G.m.b.H.: government agency for seizure of agricultural assets in the East, a division of the Reich Food Ministry

    RSHA: Reichssicherheithauptamt: Reich Security Main Office

    Sippenamt: genealogical office

    NSV: Nationalsozialistische Volkswohlfahrt, National Socialist Welfare Organization

    Arthur Greiser: Gauleiter of Wartheland (part of Poland annexed by Germany)

    Albert Forster: Gauleiter of Danzig/West Prussia (territories annexed by Germany)

    Litzmannstadt: name given to Polish city of Lodz during war
