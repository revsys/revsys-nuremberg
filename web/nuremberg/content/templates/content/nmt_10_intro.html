    NMT Case 10

    USA vs. Alfried Krupp, et al.

    Summary

    Trial X, The United States of America vs. Alfried Krupp, et al, also known as the "Krupp Trial", took place between August 1947 and July 1948; appeals of sentences continued through 1949. Twelve defendants were charged: Alfried Krupp von Bohlen und Halbach, Ewald Loeser, Eduard Houdremont, Erich Mueller, Friedrich Janssen, Karl Pfirsch, Max Ihn, Karl Eberhardt, Heinrich Korschan, Friedrich von Buelow, Werner Lehmann, and Hans Kupke. During the trial, the prosecution introduced over 1400 written exhibits; the defense introduced over 2800. These exhibits consisted of documents, photographs, affidavits, letters, and charts. Testimony was heard from over 200 witnesses. The tribunal convened 103 times over eleven months.

    This was the third and final of the so-called industrialist cases tried in Nuernberg. The defendants were the owner and leading officials in the Krupp Concern. The man who had headed the company until 1943, Gustav Krupp, the father of the first named defendant, was not indicted because of incapacity to stand trial (due to illness). He had also been indicted in the Trial of the Major War Criminals (IMT), but the Tribunal granted an application by his lawyer for postponement of the proceedings. As he did not recover, his son, who headed the firm from 1943 on, was indicted in his stead.

    Alfried Krupp and eight of the defendants were members of the Managing Board (Vorstand) of the company. From December 1943, Alfried Krupp was the sole owner and directing head of the Krupp concern. The Krupp concern was "a gigantic vertical enterprise, composed principally of coal and iron ore mines, blast furnaces, rolling mills, shipyards, machine, armament and other manufacturing plants; in 1939 it consisted of at least 175 domestic and 60 foreign subsidiary units."

    All defendants were officials of Friedrich Krupp, A.G., Essen, a firm that had been founded in 1812. The Krupp family, one of the most powerful dynasties in Europe since the 16th century, had by 1900 become widely known as Germany's principal weapons manufacturer. The company's history in modern times began with construction of a steel foundry in Essen in 1810 and the acquisition of many mines in Germany and France. The firm began to manufacture cannons by the mid 19th century. Railroad construction became another major area of business at this time, followed by shipbuilding after Krupp's acquisition of the Germania shipyards. Much of the firm's fame and fortune derived from armaments production during the Franco-Prussian War of 1870. By 1900 Krupp had become the largest private company in Germany. In 1906, they built the first German submarine. In 1943, Krupp A.G. was succeeded by an unincorporated firm, Friedrich Krupp, Essen, in accordance with a special Hitler decree. The managing body prior to 1943 was referred to as the Vorstand; after the firm's change in legal status, this body was called the "Direktorium".

    Indictment

    Count One: Crimes Against Peace

    • Invasion of other countries; wars of aggression
    • Wars in violation of international agreements
    • As the principal German maker of armament and the largest builder of U-boats and warships, Krupp contributed substantially to the ability of the Third Reich to wage wars of aggression and invade other countries

    Count Two: Crimes Against Humanity: Plunder and Spoliation

    • Plunder of private and public property
    • Spoliation and devastation of property in countries/territories occupied by Germany
    • Occupied territories exploited beyond needs of occupying army
    • Objects of plunder ranged from raw materials and agricultural products to mining and industrial enterprises taken for use by German industry

    Count Three: Crimes Against Humanity: Deportation, Exploitation, Slave Labor

    • Murder, enslavement, deportation of civilian populations
    • Enslavement of concentration camp inmates
    • Employment of prisoners of war in war-related occupations

    Count Four: Common Plan or Conspiracy

    • Formulation/execution of common plan to commit crimes against peace

    (counts one and four were dropped due to lack of evidence)

    Commonly Used Terms in Trial

    Wehrwirtschaftsfueher: military economic leader/s (six defendants)

    Vorstand: Executive board of a corporation

    Direktorium: similar to Vorstand, for unincorporated company

    Treuhandschaften: trusteeships

    Patenschaften: sponsorships

    Beschlagnahme: sequestration (of property or assets)

    Sondervermoegen: "marshalled" (confiscated) property, in USSR

    Other Significant Entities/Agencies/Organizations

    Reichsvereinigung Eisen: Reich Association Iron (RVE), official body for governance of iron and steel industries

    Reichsvereinigung Kohle: Reich Association Coal (RVK), official body for governance of the coal industry

    Rheinisch-Westfaelisches Kohlensyndicat (RWKS): subgroup of the RVK, largest coal syndicate in Germany

    Berg- und Huettenwerke Gesellschaft Ost m.b.H. (BHO): state monopoly association set up to exploit mining and smelting industries in the USSR, one of several "Ostgesellschaften," companies set up to exploit industries in the East

    Keppler Circle aka Friends of Himmler aka Freundeskreis: group of industrialists, bankers, etc who provided significant financial support to the SS

    Kleiner Kreis: Small Circle, a group exercising great influence over the coal, iron and steel industries)

    Reichswirtschaftsministerium: Ministry of Economics

    Vierjahresplan: Office of the Four-Year Plan, headed by Goering, started in 1936

    • Amt fuer Deutsche Roh- und Werkstoffe (Office for German Raw Materials and Synthetics, part of the Office of the Four-Year Plan, later known as Reichsamt fuer Wirtschaftsausbau)

    Reichsamt fuer Wirtschaftsausbau: Reich Office for Economic Development, see above

    Reichsverband der deutschen Industrie: Reich Association of German Industry, later known as Reichsgruppe Industrie

    Adolf Hitler Spende: a fund collected from German industry, banking, and agriculture, the largest private source of funds for the NSDAP, set up by Gustav Krupp in 1933

    Metallurgische Forschungsgesellschaft m.b.H. (MEFO): a "dummy corporation" set up in 1933 as a front for purchases from armament manufacturers

    Ruestungs-Vertretung ("R-Office"): Krupp's liaison office with the Wehrmacht and other military offices of the Reich

    Reichsministerium fuer Ruestung und Kriegsproduktion: Reich Ministry for Armament and Munition (Albert Speer's ministry)

    • Waffenentwicklungskommission: weapons development commission

    Wirtschaftsgruppe Eisenschaffende Industrie (WGE): Economic Group Iron Industry

    Lager-Aktion: a requisition program through which nearly 400,000 tons of iron and steel products were shipped to Germany from Holland (1942-44)

    Ruhrhilfe-Aktion: a project to dismantle Dutch factories and ship machinery to plants in the Ruhr (1944-45)

    Bofors (Aktiebolaget Bofors): Swedish company through which Krupp manufactured guns during period 1920-1935, when this was forbidden by Versailles treaty

    Rohstoffhandelsgesellschaf (ROGES): raw materials trading company
